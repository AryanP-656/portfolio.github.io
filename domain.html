Chosen Domain: Netflix

In the world of streaming services, Netflix stands as a monopoly, captivating millions of viewers worldwide with its vast library of content delivered seamlessly to screens of all sizes. Netflix is a subscription-based streaming platform offering movies, TV shows, and original content to users worldwide. Its user-friendly interface and personalized recommendations make it easy for anyone to discover and enjoy entertainment on any device.

System Design of Netflix

Functional Requirements

  • User accounts, login/logout, and subscription management
  • Video playback: play, pause, rewind, fast-forward, download
  • Personalized recommendations
  • Efficient search and autocomplete
  • Seamless streaming with CDN and buffer optimization

Non-Functional Requirements

  • Low latency and high responsiveness
  • Scalability for millions of users
  • High availability and minimal downtime
  • Secure authentication and data privacy
  • Intuitive, easy-to-use interface
System Design of Netflix (Source: geeksforgeeks.org)
Summary:
Netflix's system design leverages a microservices architecture deployed on AWS and its custom Open Connect CDN to deliver seamless, high-quality video streaming to millions of users worldwide. The client devices interact with backend microservices for user management, recommendations, and content discovery, while video streaming is handled by geographically distributed CDN servers for low latency and high availability. Content is transcoded into multiple formats and resolutions for device compatibility and network adaptability. Data storage uses a combination of MySQL for transactional data and Cassandra for scalable viewing history. The architecture emphasizes stateless services, redundancy, and real-time analytics using tools like Kafka and Spark, ensuring scalability, reliability, and a personalized user experience.
